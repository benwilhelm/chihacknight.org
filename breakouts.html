Breakout groups

Every week after the presentation, we break out into topic-specific working and learning groups led by regular facilitators to guide conversations, answer questions, and build teams for civic apps. The groups are split into two types: working groups and learning groups.

Want to start your own breakout group? Just mention it at the next hack night and people will vote with their feet!

Learning groups

Educational sessions about civic technlogy, programming, working with data and troubleshooting help.

Learning group Facilitator(s) Description
Orientation / Civic Tech 101 Christopher Whitaker New to civic technology and civic hacking? Don't even know what that means? Every week, Christopher leads quick workshop to get you up to speed. Or you can watch it online.
Code Clinic René M. Paccha Karl Fogel You bring the problems, we'll bring the experienced coders willing to take a look. Code Clinic is a group for coders — especially new coders, but open to anyone — to get a second pair of eyes on their work, debugging tips, architecture feedback, etc.
Mob Programming Nick Mader

Hands-on learning! This group shares a single screen, keyboard and mouse and use a timer to rotate control among participants to complete a shared exercise.

Dolphin Tank Mary Kuhn Civic Tech Dolphin

Develop project ideas, connect to talent and resources, or find new projects to join in a safe, friendly space with your peers. Meets the last Tuesday of every month.

Working groups

Topic-specific working groups led by facilitators to guide conversations, answer questions, and build teams for civic apps.

Topic Project Facilitator(s) Description
Environment The Naru Project Zachary Damato The Naru Project is a socially driven project that focuse on installing artifical wetlands in the Chicago River. We've installed two for plant growth, habitat restoration and growing edibles. Contact Zachary for more information.
Transportation PBR: Personalized Bike Routing Steven Vance Michael Hassin PBR will be a web service to design the best bike routes in Chicago using personalized and contributed information, avoiding streets with rough pavements and prioritizing known shortcuts.
Education IL state teacher licensure, CPS digital census Josh Kalov This group focuses mainly on Chicago Public Schools.
Health Chicago Nursing Home Search Joel Inwood This project will help Chicago area seniors or their loved ones find the best quality care available.
Environment Drek Beach Scott Beslow An Open Source, Open Data project monitoring the health of Chicago's 27 beaches.
Social Service Delivery mRelief Rose Afriyie Genevieve Nielsen The goal of the group is to develop tools to improve the delivery of social services provided by the City of Chicago.
Pensions Modelling Pension Reform in Illinois Denis Roarty Ben Galewsky Explore ways to use data and models to help pensioners and tax payers understand how reform proposals will impact them and each other.
Access to Justice Hack Night Taxi Project Kulsum Ameji

The “sharing economy” is much touted for its disruptive capabilities. Yet, many policy, legal, and regulatory issues intersect in nuanced ways in the “sharing economy.” What are the long-term implications for customers/consumers and for low-wage workers who exist in the contradictory space between traditional, highly regulated systems and new, “disruptive” economic systems? How can technology be used to enhance the functionality of these systems and to strengthen consumer and driver’s rights?
